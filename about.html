ABOUT THE PROGRAMME

Governance, ownership and the plan for Phase 1.

How Invest in Wessex is structured, who's involved, and what we're building towards over 2026/27.

GOVERNANCE & OWNERSHIP

Council-led, business-shaped.

Wiltshire Council retains overall ownership of the Invest in Wessex proposition, ensuring it stays aligned with wider economic development priorities and corporate objectives. The Council's Planning, Economy and Regeneration Directorate acts as custodian of the framework, coordinating delivery, maintaining consistency of messaging and overseeing future development of the proposition.

Successful place promotion can't be delivered by the Council alone. The credibility of Invest in Wessex depends on the active involvement of businesses, employers, investors and strategic partners who help shape and champion the county's investment story.

The Wiltshire Business Forum acts as the principal business advisory group — bringing together major employers, investors and strategic business representatives to test propositions, understand market perceptions, identify emerging opportunities, and keep the programme informed by real-world business experience. The Forum doesn't manage delivery; its role is to provide strategic insight, constructive challenge and business-led advice.

THE FORUM'S ROLE

In practical terms, the Forum can:

Provide feedback on the Invest in Wessex proposition and key messages.

Identify opportunities to strengthen the county's investment offer.

Act as a sounding board for future campaigns and initiatives.

Highlight investment barriers and emerging business needs.

Champion Wiltshire through member networks, supply chains and industry connections.

Act as ambassadors for the Invest in Wessex proposition.

PHASE 1 DELIVERY PLAN · 2026/27

Establishing the foundations.

Phase 1 focuses on defining the narrative, creating core communications assets, engaging stakeholders and building the organisational capability needed to support future investment promotion. The objective is a clear, credible and evidence-based proposition that Wiltshire Council, businesses, partners and stakeholders can all consistently communicate.

1. Establish the proposition

Core narrative, messaging and positioning — brand proposition, key messages, audience segmentation, sector narratives, communications framework, and a visual identity toolkit.

2. Develop core content & assets

Reusable materials across platforms and audiences — prospectus, sector overviews, an investor presentation deck, case studies, photography, a content library and visual assets.

3. Digital presence

A professional, credible gateway rather than a complex standalone platform — dedicated webpages, sector content, business case studies, an enquiry mechanism and content templates.

4. Stakeholder & business engagement

Embedding the proposition with key audiences — Business Forum engagement, partner briefings, a business ambassador programme, partner toolkit and engagement events.

5. Future pipeline development

Preparing for future investment attraction — identifying target sectors and investors, reviewing available sites and premises, testing the proposition, and planning Phase 2 communications.

BY THE END OF 2026/27

Wiltshire will have:

• A clearly defined investment proposition.
• An agreed brand narrative and messaging framework.
• A suite of professional investment materials.

• A digital presence for Invest in Wessex.
• Strong engagement with businesses and partners.
• A pipeline of future investment promotion activity.
